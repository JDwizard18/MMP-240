"The Duel"/ "Dyueru"

Studio: Kamikaze Douga, Directed by Takanobu Mizuno,Written by Takashi Okazaki

The episode is set in an alternate history Star Wars univers where 20 years after a war between the Feudal-Jedi Empire and a renegade Jedi sect called the Sith, a lone wanderer known only as the "Ronin" witnesses a legion of former stormtroopers attempt to besiege a small village and reluctantlt decides to take action.

"Tatooine Rhapsody"/"Tatuīn Rapusodi "

Studio: Studio Colorido, Directed by Taku Kimura, Written by Yasumi Atarashi

During the Clone Wars, a Padawan named Jay attempts to escape order 66 only to stumble upon a Hutt named Gee who offers to take Jay in if he becomes the lead singer of his rock band the Star Wavers. Years later, during the reign of the Galactic Empire, the band is being hunted by the bounty hunter Boba Fett, who was hired by Gee's uncle Jabba becuase Gee refused to join him in the family business.

"The Twins"/ "Tsuinzu"

Studio: Studio Trigger, Directed by Hiroyuki Imaishi, Written by Hiromi Wakabayashi

In the aftermath of the Battle of Exegol, the remnants of the First Order and Sith Eternal join to create Force-sensitive twins named Karre and Am, who they then train in the dark side of the Force. Additionally they construct a conjoined Star Destroyers that house a large super laser capable of destroying planets. The twins are made the leaders of the remnants and planed to use their superweapon to destroy the New Republic, however, Karre goes rogue on the day the weapon is supposed to be fired due to him having a vision of the future involving his sister's death. He goes on to steals the large kyber crystal that powers the superweapon ultimately leading to a confrontation with his sister..

"The Village Bride"/"Mura no Hanayome"

Studio: Kinema Citrus, Directed by Hitoshi Haga, Written by Takahito Oonishi & Hitoshi Haga

Years after the Great Jedi Purge, a former Jedi Padawan named F is drawn to a remote planet by an explorer named Valco. Valco explains that bandit raiders have reprogrammed old Separatist battle droids and are holding a village hostage. The village chief's granddaughter Haru and her fiancé Asu intend to surrender to the bandits as collateral the following morning, while Haru's sister Saku wants to fight the bandits. Moved by the villagers actions and memories of her master who called this planet home, F steps up as a Jedi to defend the villagers from the raiders.

"The Ninth Jedi"/"Kyūninme no Jedai"

Studio: Production I.G, Directed by Kenji Kamiyama, Written by Kenji Kamiyama

Many generations after the Jedi Order became mostly extinct, Margrave Juro invites seven Jedi to his aerial temple to receive lightsabers, whose design has been lost to time. Juro's droid gifts a lightsaber to one of the Jedi, Ethan, and promises that the others will arrive shortly. On the planet Hy Izlan below, the sabersmith Lah Zhima finishes constructing the remaining lightsabers, including one for his Force-sensitive daughter, Kara, before being captured by hunters working for the Sith. What ensues is a conflict that can serve as the beginning of an epic adventure.

"T0-B1"

Studio: Production Science SARU Directed by Abel Góngora, Written by Yuichiro Kido

Set after the Great Jedi Purge, our story follows a droid named T0-B1 (Tobi) lives on a deserted planet with his armless creator Professor Mitaka. Our droid protagonist dreams of becoming a Jedi Knight so Mitaka tells him that in order to become a Jedi, he must find a kyber crystal so he can forge a lightsaber. T0-B1 goes on to scours the planet, but finds nothing so he decides to defy Mitaka's orders to never enter his basement. In doing so he discovers a starship, and accidentally sends a signal that alerts a Sith Inquisitor to their presence.
