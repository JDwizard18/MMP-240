Star Wars: Visions is an animated anthology series of non-canon short films created for the streaming service, Disney+. It's initial release was comprised of nine original short films from various well known Japanese animation studios that present a different cultural perspective of Star Wars and it is all done in the various anime styles each studio is well known for. The series premiered on September 22, 2021 with an initial running that proved very successful. Shortly after it'.s realease the first episode titled “The Duel” was used as the bases of a novel called ”Ronin” telling the story of the protagonist of the episode in greater detail.

On May 29, 2022, a 2nd volume of Star Wars: Visions was announced with a focus on showcasing animation studios from around the world featuring a wide variety of animation styles and cultural references and styling. The 2nd volume debuted on May 4, 2023 on Disney+ with the same accompanying featurette as the first volume, and proved to be similar success. The success of the fist two volumes has lead talks of a third season but as of yet nothing has been officially announced.
